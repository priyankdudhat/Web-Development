Abstract

Industry 4.0 is a strategic initiative which the German government has introduced. Which outlines and encapsulates a set of technological changes in manufacturing and sets out the goals of a comprehensive policy agenda with a view to preserving the global competitiveness of the German industry. Industry 4.0 has purchased a range of professional improvements in the working strategies of workers. Introduction

We have been surrounded by advanced technologies that transform every aspect of our lives, as well as the production manufacturing processes. From the first British industrial revolution to the fourth industrial revolution, there has been dramatic changes through digitalization which change the humans lifestyle and communication style with each others.[1]

The First Industrial Revolution begun from 1780s with introduction of mechanical production facilities with the help of water and steam power. Next Industrial revolution 2.0 – “Electrification and Industrialization” defined as a mass production, For example steel revolution in manufacturing. In this revolution period advent of the assembly line, electrical power , telephone, refrigerator as well as washing machines.

At the time of 20th century, Industry 3.0 begun with the introduction of the digital revolution. This revolution witnessed exponential technology like 3D Printing, Big Data, Internet and huge development in Computers, Information and Communication technology industries. KEY CHARACTERISTICS OF INDUSTRY 4.0

  • Big Data Analytics:
    In the context of Industry 4.0, big data is a helpful for the companies to do prediction of production in the future and this technology important factor for rapid and fast development of industries though the IoT. This leads to a huge amount of information produced and obtained daily, where the current processing and analysis cannot be carried out using traditional methods. Big data has therefore recently become a hot topic in I4.0.Several other applications would be able to acquire additional value if existing techniques were to become more advanced in the ability to handle of big data. Big data is the use of digital technologies for analysis purposes. As defined by Forrester, ”Big Data” can be divided into four dimensions: volume, variety, value and velocity.[2][5]

  • B. Autonomous Robots:
    Currently available robots are constructed with more advanced functionalities and more flexible as well as easy to handle by normal people. In the short time of period robots will interact with each other and actively do collaborations with the humans in the advise of the managers. In the feature these robots will become a more and more cheaper and advanced to achieve the position in the industries.[2][5]

  • C. Simulation:
    Simulation is one more important aspects in Industry 4.0, which helps to developed well planned and experimental models to optimize and rebuilt the complex operations and automated production systems. Simulation architecture build of using currently available data to represent the original world and this model is combination of workers, products and different machineries. Simulation concept is also know as virtual reality. For example, now driving schools have a simulation machine for virtual driving.[2][5]

  • D. Horizontal and Vertical System Integration:
    Vertical integration refers to flexible and reconfigurable systems within the factory and to the extent to which they are fully integrated with each other to achieve agility; horizontal integration deals with the integration of partners within the Supply Chain(SC). The industrial network collects Big Data in order to optimize the performance of the system and send it to the cloud. This coordination mechanism is the framework of the smart factory. Manufacturing systems are therefore designed as a self-organized structure that integrates all physical objects into each other through smart networks. In addition, cloud-based systems enable vertical partners to integrate with each other through shared platforms. Product and process flows would be visualized and monitored by SC members (Wang et al., 2016).[6]

  • E. Internet of Things (IoT):
    IoT refers to the next technological revolution by providing solutions for computing, analytics, etc., and relying on cloudbased systems. The main task of IoT is to connect to the Internet by collecting data from physical objects. By collecting data, computers or higher-level devices make an operational decision. By using IoT, business operations become more agile and integrated, as well as competitive advantage on the basis of SC.

  • F. The Cloud Computing:
    Cloud Computing (CC) brings different advantages to the ICT paradigm, such as helping SCs automate and integrate, but also facilitating management and administration. It’s a way to virtualize resources and services and to combine a client/server-based system. CC includes a pool of IT resources that offer storage and processing capabilities in a virtual system by serving multiple users. There are three models of cloud computing; first one is Software as a Service (SaaS) where access depends on customer purchases such as ERP, second model is Service (PaaS) where customers are allowed to access their cloud applications such as software developers and third model Infrastructure as a Service (IaaS) offers basic activities such as storage capabilities. The best example of cloud systems are Google Drive, Windows Azure and Blue Cloud.[2][5]

Six Sigma principals
  1. Interoperability: The ability of Cyber-Physical Systems, Humans and Smart Factories to connect and communicate with each other via the Internet of Things and the Internet of Services.
  2. Virtualization: A virtual copy of the Smart Factory which is created by linking sensor data (from monitoring physical processes) with virtual plant models and simulation models.
  3. Decentralization: The ability of Cyber-Physical Systems within Smart Factories to make decisions on their own.
  4. Service Orientation: Service Orientation: Offering of services (of cyber-physical systems, humans and Smart Factories) via the Internet of Services.
  5. Real-Time Capability: The capability to collect and analyze data and provide the insights immediately.
  6. Modularity: Flexible adaptation of Smart Factories for changing requirements of individual modules.
